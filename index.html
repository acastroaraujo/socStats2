Resources

These are my personal recommendations for resources to start getting interested with causal inference.

Class Resources:

  • The Effect by NHK (Huntington-Klein 2021)

Short:

  • Research questions and estimands (Lundberg et al. 2021)

  • Introduction to DAGs (Rohrer 2018; Cinelli et al. 2022)

    The Causal Diagrams edX course by Miguel Hernán is pretty good too.

  • Don’t interpret “control” or “adjustment” variables (Westreich and Greenland 2013; Keele et al. 2020; Hünermund and Louw 2023).

  • The effects of seemingly immutable characteristics (Sen and Wasow 2016; cf. Holland 1986).

Good to play around with:

  • Counterfactuals and Causal Inference (Morgan and Winship 2014)

    I’ve been told many times that the first edition is better, so maybe try that one instead.

  • Causal Inference: The Mixtape (Cunningham 2021)

    Covers a lot of the same ground as NHK, but has more math. It is also written by an economist.

  • Regression and Other Stories (Gelman et al. 2020)

    Chapters 18-21

  • Theory & Credibility (Ashworth et al. 2021)

Advanced Resources:

  • Mostly Harmless Econometrics (Angrist and Pischke 2009)

  • Causality: Models, Reasoning and Inference (Pearl 2009)

  • Causal Inference: What If (Hernan and Robins 2023)

    This book gets used a lot in epidemiology.
